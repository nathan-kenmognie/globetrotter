What Is Cameroon?

Cameroon is a country in Central Africa known for its diverse landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant wildlife. Often called "Africa in miniature," it features beaches, deserts, mountains, rainforests, and savannas. Cameroon is home to over 250 ethnic groups, each with its own traditions, languages, and cuisines, making it a unique and fascinating destination for travelers.

What Foods Can You Find?

Cameroon offers a wide variety of delicious foods influenced by its many cultures.
Some popular dishes include:

  • Ndolé: A stew made with bitter leaves, peanuts, and meat or fish.
  • Jollof Rice: Spiced rice cooked with tomatoes, onions, and vegetables, often served with chicken or fish.
  • Poulet DG: Chicken cooked with plantains, vegetables, and spices
  • Eru: A leafy green vegetable dish cooked with waterleaf, palm oil, and meat or fish.
  • Koki: Steamed bean cake made from black-eyed peas and palm oil.
  • Suya: Spicy grilled meat skewers, popular as street food.
  • Achu: Pounded cocoyams served with a yellow soup made from palm oil and spices.
  • Fufu and Njama Njama: Cornmeal dough served with sautéed huckleberry leaves.
  • Mbongo Tchobi: Fish or meat cooked in a rich, dark sauce made from burnt spices.
